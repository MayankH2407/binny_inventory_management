What We Are Building

The Basiq360 Inventory Management Application is a QR-code-driven, mobile-first digital system designed to give Binny Footwear real-time visibility and full traceability over their footwear inventory — from the factory floor to warehouse dispatch.

The system introduces a two-level carton hierarchy: Child Boxes (inner cartons) with permanent QR identities and Master Cartons (outer boxes) with dynamic QR identities that can be created, closed, and recreated as per operational needs — all while keeping inventory records perfectly reconciled.

Solution Objectives

Objective Description
Eliminate Inventory MismatchesDigitally track every pack, unpack, and re-pack cycle so physical stock always matches system records.
Persistent Child Box TrackingEvery inner carton carries a permanent QR code that retains its identity regardless of how many master cartons it moves through.
Controlled Master Carton LifecycleMaster cartons are digitally created, activated, closed (on unpacking), and re-created — each as a distinct entity with a unique QR code.
Real-Time Inventory UpdatesEvery scan event — pack, unpack, dispatch — instantly updates the central inventory database.
Full Audit TraceabilityHistorical lineage of every child box across all master cartons it has ever been part of is permanently retained.
Minimal Operational DisruptionMobile-phone-based scanning with no conveyor belts or fixed hardware required in Phase 1.

Section 02
Background & Problem Statement

Current Operational Reality at Binny Footwear

Binny Footwear (Mahavir Polymers Pvt. Ltd.) operates a factory and warehouse where footwear is manufactured, packed, stored, and dispatched. The existing process relies heavily on manual labelling — article name, colour, size, and quantity are handwritten on master carton boxes.

While this works at small volumes, it creates significant data integrity issues as operations scale — particularly around a recurring operational pattern: master cartons being unpacked and re-packed multiple times before final dispatch.

The Core Problem: Inventory Mismatch

The Unpack-Repack Gap

When a master carton is physically unpacked and its child boxes re-arranged into a new master carton, the system has no record of this event. The old master carton remains "active" in records while its contents have moved — creating phantom stock entries and inventory counts that don't match the warehouse floor.

Impact of the Problem

Impact AreaConsequence
Stock Count AccuracyPhysical count and system count diverge — making reports unreliable
Dispatch ErrorsRisk of dispatching already-empty or re-used master cartons
Audit TrailNo historical record of which child boxes moved between which master cartons
Decision MakingManagement cannot trust inventory data for production planning or order fulfillment

Why This Engagement Was Revised

The original scope of the Basiq360 system addressed packing and scanning but did not include a digital mechanism for the unpack event. During operational review discussions between both teams, this gap was identified and agreed upon as a critical addition. The scope was revised to include a fully controlled Pack → Scan → Unpack → Re-Pack digital lifecycle for master cartons.

Agreed Resolution

Both Basiq360 and Binny Footwear teams agreed to digitally enforce the carton lifecycle — ensuring that no child box can exist in more than one active master carton at any point in time, and that all unpack events are logged and inventory updated instantly.

Section 04
Process Flow — Box Packing & QR Linking
The standard packing and scanning workflow that forms the foundation of the system

4.1   Box Packing

  • Inner (child) boxes are packed first at the production stage
  • QR code labels are generated in volume based on the production plan
  • Each label is printed and pasted on the corresponding child box during packing
  • Multiple child boxes (with potentially mixed assortments) are then packed into a single master carton

4.2   QR Code Association

  • During packing, the warehouse operator scans each child box QR code using the mobile app
  • Once all required child boxes are scanned (based on configured box count), the system automatically generates a unique parent QR code for the master carton
  • The parent QR code is digitally linked to: all child box QR codes, assortment details, and total count
  • A master carton label is printed and pasted on the outer box

4.3   Mobile-Based Scanning

  • The packed master box is scanned using a standard mobile phone camera
  • Works on both Android and iOS devices
  • No conveyor belt or fixed barcode scanner hardware required in Phase 1
  • App works in low-connectivity environments with offline queue support

4.4   Label Printing

  • An external label printer is connected to the mobile phone
  • Connectivity options: USB cable or Bluetooth
  • After scanning, operator taps "Print Label" in the app
  • Label prints instantly with full assortment details

4.5   Inventory Validation

  • On scanning the master box QR code, the app shows complete carton contents
  • Box count is displayed and can be verified against physical count
  • Inventory status is updated in real time in the central system
  • On dispatch: master box QR is scanned → marked as Dispatched → warehouse stock decremented

Section 05
Revised Process Flow
Pack → Scan → Unpack → Re-Pack: The controlled digital carton lifecycle
⚠ Why This Was Added
The original scope only covered Pack and Scan. Real-world operations at Binny Footwear require master cartons to be frequently opened and child boxes rearranged. Without digital control of the unpack event, inventory records drifted from physical reality. This revised flow adds controlled Unpack and Re-Pack steps.

5.1   Master Carton Packing

  • Operator scans free child boxes using the mobile application
  • System validates: is this child box already linked to an active parent?
  • If validation passes, system generates a new master carton QR code
  • All scanned child boxes are digitally linked to this new parent QR
  • Master carton label with full assortment details is printed and pasted

5.2   Master Carton Scan & Storage

  • After pasting the label, the master carton QR is scanned
  • System marks this master carton as ACTIVE
  • Inventory count is updated in real time at the warehouse/factory level
  • Master carton is now ready for storage or dispatch

5.3   Master Carton Unpacking (Critical Step)

  • Before physically opening a master carton, operator must scan its QR code
  • Operator selects "Unpack Master Carton" in the mobile app
  • System immediately executes three actions:
    1. Parent carton status set to UNPACKED / CLOSED
    2. All linked child boxes released as free inventory
    3. Inventory records updated instantly
  • The closed master carton QR cannot be reactivated or dispatched

5.4   Re-Packing into New Master Carton

  • Released child boxes (now free inventory) can be re-scanned
  • Operator packs them into a new physical master carton
  • System generates a brand new parent QR code
  • Full carton lineage is retained — all previous master cartons this child box was part of are recorded
  • Process loops back to Step 5.1

Section 06
Inventory Validation & Controls
System rules that guarantee 100% reconciliation between physical and digital inventory
# Control Rule System Behaviour Business Impact
1 One Active Parent Per Child Box A child box QR code can be linked to only one active master carton at any point in time. The system rejects any scan that attempts to add an already-linked child box to a new parent without first unpacking the current parent. Eliminates duplicate inventory counts. The same physical box cannot be counted in two places simultaneously.
2 Unpacked Cartons are Permanently Closed Once a master carton is unpacked (status set to UNPACKED/CLOSED), its QR code cannot be reactivated, dispatched, or associated with any new child boxes. The code is permanently retired. Prevents phantom inventory — old QR codes cannot be accidentally re-scanned and counted.
3 Mandatory Pre-Unpack Scan Before any physical unpacking, the parent QR code MUST be scanned and the "Unpack" action confirmed in the app. The system will not release child boxes without this step. Forces every unpack event to be digitally recorded — no silent unpacking that leaves ghost records.
4 Historical Lineage Retained The system permanently stores the relationship between every child box and every master carton it has ever been part of — with timestamps for each pack and unpack event. Full audit trail for investigations, returns, or compliance. Any child box's complete journey is traceable.
5 Real-Time Inventory Reconciliation Every scan event — pack, unpack, re-pack, dispatch — triggers an immediate update to the central inventory count. No batch or end-of-day sync is required. Inventory figures are always current. Management can check stock levels at any time and trust the numbers.
6 Dispatch Gate Check Only master cartons with ACTIVE status can be dispatched. Cartons that are UNPACKED/CLOSED or that have mismatched child box counts cannot be dispatched. Prevents dispatch of empty or partially-filled cartons. Every dispatch is verified against system records.
📈 Net Result: 100% Physical-to-System Reconciliation

These six controls working together ensure that at any point in time, the sum of inventory in the Basiq360 system will exactly match the physical stock present on the warehouse floor — regardless of how many pack/unpack/re-pack cycles have occurred.

Assumptions & Dependencies
Shared understanding of what both teams are relying on for a successful delivery
📌 Why This Section Matters
Assumptions are things we believe to be true and have designed around. If any assumption turns out to be incorrect, it may impact scope, timeline, or cost. Please review and flag any disagreements during the kickoff call.

Assumptions — Technical

#
Assumption
1
Stable Internet at Packing/Warehouse Area
The system requires internet connectivity (Wi-Fi or mobile data) during scanning operations. If connectivity is intermittent, an offline queue mechanism will be required — this should be confirmed in Week 1.
2
Label Printer Compatibility
The external label printer selected by Binny Footwear will support standard thermal printing drivers compatible with the Basiq360 mobile application. Printer model to be confirmed by Week 2.
3
Master Data Ready Before Week 3
The complete list of article names, colour codes, and size ranges currently in production will be provided by Binny team in Excel/CSV format by end of Week 2 for upload into the system.
4
Mobile Device Availability
At least one dedicated Android/iOS device will be available and assigned to the packing/warehouse team for app usage during Phase 1. Multiple devices can be configured if needed.
5
No ERP Integration in Phase 1
The Basiq360 inventory system will operate as a standalone application. Integration with any existing ERP, accounting, or dispatch software is not in scope for Phase 1 and will be assessed separately.

Assumptions — Operational

6
Operator Compliance with Pre-Unpack Scanning
Warehouse operators will consistently scan the master carton QR code and trigger the "Unpack" action in the app BEFORE physically opening any master carton. This is a critical process discipline requirement. Failure to do so will cause inventory mismatches. Training will cover this.
7
QR Labels Will Not Be Damaged or Removed
Child box QR labels are expected to remain intact and scannable throughout the carton's lifecycle. Damaged or missing labels will require a re-labelling workflow (can be handled by the system but must be flagged immediately).
8
Dedicated UAT Period (Week 7)
Binny Footwear team will allocate time in Week 7 (Apr 20–26) for User Acceptance Testing. At least one supervisor and one packing operator should participate. Feedback must be consolidated and submitted within 3 business days of testing.
9
Single Location — Phase 1
Phase 1 covers a single factory/warehouse location (Jaipur, Rajasthan). Multi-location or inter-warehouse transfer functionality is not in scope and will be planned separately if required.
10
Change Management by Binny Team
Binny Footwear management will communicate the new digital scanning process to all packing and warehouse staff before go-live. Basiq360 will provide training support but team adoption is a shared responsibility.
